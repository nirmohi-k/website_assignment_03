Nirmohi Hemant Kathrecha

Hi! I'm Nirmohi.

I am a licensed architect and designer working at the intersection of urbanism, climate change, and equity. My work focuses on spatial justice, housing studies, and the urban commons.

Currently, I am studying at MS Advanced Urban Design at Cornell University in New York. My previous projects have examined urban flooding, density, and sea-level rise in Mumbai, and I have worked with local communities and policymakers to propose interventions.

My professional experience includes working with SJK Architects on sustainability and material lifecycle research and contributing to designing adaptive urban spaces. With a keen interest in research and writing, I have also been featured in Unbuilt 2.0: Architecture of the Future Collectives, Mid-Day and Citizen Matters for my research on climate change and mapping methodologies.

Power Structures in Educational Institutions

As published in Unbuilt 2.0, the design creates fantastical punctuations in the ‘traditional’ framework of the building. It metaphorically reifies the notion of ‘play’, of a parallel liberalisation from the conventional and the older zeitgeist. The ambition is to provoke, evoke, to create a self-referential idea that breaks away from the known and the latent. Instead of formal and material aspects, it focuses on a choreography of experiences that bring in this point of view.
